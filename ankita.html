1 • Smart Traffic Automation & Incident Detection System
Graph · Segment Tree
Dijkstra · A* · KMP

Aarunya's traffic system processes live camera feeds and sensor data across 500+ intersections to detect accidents, breakdowns and unusual congestion patterns in real-time. Road networks form weighted graphs where Dijkstra finds fastest emergency routes while A* handles dynamic obstacles for ambulances. Segment trees aggregate traffic density across road segments for instant range queries, and KMP scans license plate sequences to identify repeat offenders or stolen vehicles automatically.

Aligned SDGs: SDG 11 – Sustainable Cities and Communities, SDG 9 – Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure

Data Structures: adjacency list, segment tree, priority_queue — Algorithms: Dijkstra, A* search, KMP pattern matching
Camera Feeds → KMP → License Plate Matches → Alert List Traffic Density → Segment Tree → Congestion Ranges Emergency Calls → Graph + A*/Dijkstra → Fastest Routes

2 • Aarunya Smart Water–Energy Optimization Grid
Graph · Priority Queue
Kruskal · Knapsack DP · BFS

The water-energy grid balances pumping costs against storage capacity across Aarunya's reservoirs, treatment plants and distribution pipes during peak demand. Kruskal builds minimum-cost pipe networks connecting water sources to high-demand zones, while knapsack DP optimally fills reservoirs given limited pumping energy budgets. BFS validates network connectivity during maintenance shutdowns to ensure no neighborhood loses service entirely.

Aligned SDGs: SDG 6 – Clean Water and Sanitation, SDG 7 – Affordable and Clean Energy

Data Structures: vector<Edge>, priority_queue, UnionFind — Algorithms: Kruskal MST, 0/1 Knapsack DP, BFS connectivity
Pipe Proposals → Kruskal + UnionFind → Optimal Network Daily Demand → Knapsack DP → Reservoir Fill Plan Maintenance Zones → BFS → Service Continuity Check

3 • Intelligent Waste Collection & Recycling Network
Graph · Priority Queue
TSP Heuristic · Dijkstra · Hash Tables

Waste collection trucks serve 2,500+ bins across Aarunya with fixed capacity and time windows, maximizing collection while minimizing fuel. Travelling Salesman Problem heuristics generate efficient routes between high-priority overflowing bins, while Dijkstra reroutes trucks around traffic or road closures. Hash tables track bin fill levels and material types for optimal truck loading and sorting plant assignments.

Aligned SDGs: SDG 11 – Sustainable Cities and Communities, SDG 12 – Responsible Consumption and Production

Data Structures: adjacency matrix, unordered_map — Algorithms: TSP nearest neighbor, Dijkstra, hash lookups
Bin Sensors → Hash Table → Fill Levels + Priority High Priority Bins → TSP Heuristic → Truck Routes Live Traffic → Dijkstra → Route Adjustments
